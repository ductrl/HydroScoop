Innovation

We aim to tackle two issues: provide renewable energy and keep the sewerage clean. To achieve this, we divided it into two main components: a power generator and a fishnet-like mechanism that acts as the cleaner.

The product is specifically designed for sewers on street corners in Ho Chi Minh City. The net mechanism will be installed at the front, completely covering the sewer mouth, along with a rotating component. The net would rotate periodically in order to remove any litter present, with a litter box on top, concentrating them into one area for ease of collection. A water turbine is installed inside a sewer pipe below and harnesses the kinetic energy from water flow, and transfers it through a series of gears to turn the generator, producing electricity. The power produced will be allocated among two paths: charging the batteries and powering the trash-removing mechanism, both monitored by an Arduino motherboard. The batteries will serve as a backup resolution for times when the mechanism requires a higher amount of energy or when the system goes into hibernation during the dry season.

Diving deeper, the unpredictable nature of rains in Vietnam forces us to be innovative and come up with an adaptive design that would work in various water flows. Firstly, we conducted on-field investigations and learned from some city-planning executives about the structure of the sewerage. The most common pipe diameter is 300mm, with a minimum flow velocity of around 0.8m/s, and can get up to 2m/s during the rainy season. Through calculations, accounting for the regulated filling ratio up to 0.6 of the pipe diameter, there are as many as 40W of power readily available from usual sewerage activity. This provides a steady energy flow at all times, ensuring a consistent power output. However, considering a few extreme flooding cases happen during the year, preventing system overload is necessary. Therefore, we arrived at a solution similar to that in our day-to-day motorbikes – a gear shifter. This idea utilises the principle of torques and gear ratios to ensure a steady energy input to the generator, and also to prevent overload in extreme floods. After extensive research, we saw the potential of this solution, especially through a research that quoted "The power output increased by nearly five times".

Furthermore, many newspapers have revealed the health risks that public cleaners have to face when they clean the sewerage, spending 4-6 hours each day cleaning matters ranging from exposed used needles to possible diseases from the decomposing dead animals. In addition, there have been many occasions where extreme floods were caused due to trash blocking the sewers, affecting the whole district and posing death risks because of exposed power lines. Therefore, considering the 80,000 sewers around the city that require thousands of hours of intensive labor to maintain, the application of the product will not just provide renewable energy to the community, but also provide a new solution in keeping the sewerage clean without human labor, ensuring people’s health and well-being. This is a critical point, as blockages happen in many other developing countries such as India, Bangladesh or Myanmar, and they all share a common point of risking public cleaners’ health to keep the sewerage clean. To humankind as a whole, ensuring people’s health and safety is the goal, to which we think that our product is able to achieve that.

However, we are also mindful of reverse-engineering and the competitiveness of markets both domestically and internationally. Under the Vietnamese government copyright law, our product design will be protected for up to 20 years, which would allow us to develop our market share safely in the domestic markets. Furthermore, to prevent foreign competitors from reverse-engineering and access to our product, we would be involved closely with the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment, and the Ministry of Construction to receive a higher level of protection due to involvement in governmental programs.

Innovation Brief

Elevator pitch
Through HydroScoop, we endeavor to be at the forefront of harnessing energy from an unconventional yet potent natural force – floods. Our goal is to leverage this energy to power our machine designed to prevent waste from entering sewerage and possibly serve as an inventive method to power homes. In embracing such a strategic approach, we seek to revolutionize the field and to address key sustainable development goals relating to sustainable cities and communities. By delivering clean electricity and mitigating sewage blockages, this enhances the well-being of residents in waterlogged urban areas.

With the current surge in sustainable ventures and a heightened focus on global goals, we perceive this as an auspicious business prospect. This offers an opportunity with heightened access to funding and an expanding international market featuring an increasingly discerning customer base. This augments our product development and helps facilitate effective marketing to customers, ensuring a reliable revenue stream.
Validation/progress
Many field surveys and research have also been conducted to gain a better understanding of the true demand for our product. After inspecting many sewers in various areas around the city that have been reported to be blocked during floods, we gained more insight on how to design an effective net that would be able to capture litter while allowing water to flow through. In addition, we reached out to public cleaners to hear about their experience in cleaning sewerage, many have pointed out the need to improve the methods by which the sewages are cleaned. This validates our innovation as we are effectively attempting to solve the issue faced by people in the community.

In terms of product development, we managed to simulate how the electricity would be produced by turning a 775 12V DC motor as the generator with an attached 3D-printed turbine using water. We tested it under controlled-ideal conditions using a faucet, pointing all the water at the blade at a constant rate, and were able to calculate the efficiency to be around 40%. Despite this being a very low efficiency compared to the average hydro-turbine efficiency of ~90% (Department of Interior Bureau Reclamation, 2005), we view it as the stepping stone to develop the rest of the product as our gear-shift system has yet to be developed due to the rigorous 3D-modelling and extensive engineering that it requires, in addition to the lack of lubrication in the motor itself leading to high energy loss. Currently, the Arduino programming is also in progress where we are figuring out how to use it to allocate the power flow to different paths. Moreover, we are looking at the possibilities of controlling the unit from a distance by connecting it to a Wi-fi network. Furthermore, with information gained from on-field surveys, we tested different mesh sizes on their ability to capture litter and also the draining ability. We figured that the mesh number of 5 per square inch, about 4mm wide per hole, is most suitable as it can capture litters as small as a syringe, a common litter found in sewers, and virtually has no resistance to the water flow.
Competition
In the bustling urban landscape of Vietnam, where rapid development often collides with environmental challenges, a new frontier in sewage management has emerged. The competition for a sewage clearing product has intensified, with the current market dominated by a solution that only addresses a fraction of the issue. The existing competitor's product, while effective to some extent, clears only 30-50% of the clogged sewage. This leaves a significant gap in meeting the increasing demands posed by growing urban populations and expanding infrastructure. In response to this, our product not only aims to outperform the competition by ensuring the complete removal of sewage blockages but also introduces an innovative approach to sustainable energy.

Our cutting-edge solution goes beyond traditional sewage clearance by relying solely on the water flow within the sewers as the main energy source. This singular functionality not only addresses the critical issue of sewage blockages but also aligns with contemporary sustainability efforts. By converting the kinetic energy of the flowing water into a primary power source, our product not only reduces the reliance on conventional energy grids but also contributes to the broader goal of eco-friendly urban development.

As urban areas in Vietnam continue to expand, the strain on sewage systems is escalating, and the need for a comprehensive solution has never been more pressing. In this competitive landscape, our sewage clearing product emerges not just as a remedy for immediate concerns but as a catalyst for sustainable urban development. With the potential to clear all sewage blockages and utilize a sustainable energy source, our product strives to redefine the standards in sewage management, setting a new paradigm for efficiency, environmental responsibility, and urban progress in Vietnam.
Opportunity
In Ho Chi Minh City, there are more than 80,000 sewers, all of which are constantly getting filled with loads of litter and filthy liquid every day, requiring manual cleaning that takes a lengthy time, disturbs traffic as well as pedestrians, and is known to have one of the worst working conditions.

Reportedly, cleaners have to face various risks such as skin diseases and wounds, respiratory diseases, fatal accidents, and many more caused by harsh working conditions. These conditions only worsen during flood seasons, when the amount of trash accumulated in such sewers increases significantly and the weather furthers difficulties.

As such, this is where we wanted engineering to step in: To bring a solution that can automate the process of sewer cleaning. Our product HydroScoop tackles such challenges with two essential abilities: automation and adaptability.

Vietnam has long been notorious for its extreme weather and unpredictability, frequently ranking among the countries most exposed to extreme weather. But astoundingly, this is where HydroScoop thrives. We included a hydroelectricity generator using water flow in the sewage to power the product, which allows it not only notably to decrease human contact for sewer cleaning but also provides a new reliable source of renewable energy.
Market
We intend to target local government agencies and government bodies responsible for public works. Local government agencies strive to keep their cities clean to improve quality of life and property value. Government bodies such as the Ministry of Construction and the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment oversee public works including sewages and canals. Sewage overflow would create economic and environmental issues, lower public image and community livability, and damage the canal system and surrounding infrastructure over time. However, current solutions to sewage overflow require too much manpower and aren’t efficient in intense weather. Local agencies and government bodies need a low-maintenance, effective, and environmentally friendly solution to this growing problem. Since HCMC is a densely populated area, public health and livability are top concerns for the government here. Around 60-70 billion VND is spent each year in HCMC to repair the canal system. The Ministry of Planning and Investment allocated an extra 17,5 million USD to the Japanese Government in 2023 to repair the obsolete sewage system of the city. The buyer and payer would be slightly different in this situation. The buyer is the finance department that makes the payment, but the customers benefiting and utilizing the product are government agencies. In this industry ecosystem, we have the local government agencies and government bodies as the buyer/customer. We would have to use bidding platforms and legal services related to government contracts as a bridge to our buyers. We would rely on sewer cleaning equipment manufacturers as our suppliers. Regulatory bodies, environmental organizations, and the community directly affected by sewage overflow could advocate for the use of our product. Regulatory bodies will also make sure our product meets the standards for wastewater management.
Go-to-market
We will participate in government tenders and post RFPs through the Ministry of Planning and Investment National bidding network system to sell our product. Through market research, we know two aspects most important to government projects are timeliness and affordability; thus, we will align our bids with these priorities. Additionally, we’ll collaborate with current government contractors in wastewater management, such as T-Tech Vietnam Technology Group, to include our products in their bidding with government agencies. Apart from government contractors, we’ll form strategic partnerships with organizations that align with our cause to promote our product, for example, the Vietnam Green Building Council - a Vietnamese Social Enterprise that encourages green building. Our pilot customers would be government agencies in densely populated cities, hence most affected by the consequences of sewer overflow. This includes municipal governments in Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi…This way, we could demonstrate the product’s efficiency in solving urgent problems.
